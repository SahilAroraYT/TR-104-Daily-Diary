# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Training evaluators and mentors reviewing a 22-week data science and machine learning training program. Their primary context is assessment: they need to quickly scan what was covered each week, access supporting materials (notes, assignments, external links), and verify the depth and breadth of the training. Secondary users include the trainee themselves for portfolio/self-reference.

## Product Purpose

Organize and present 22 weeks of Python, ML, DL, Power BI, and Dataiku training materials in a browsable, professional timeline. Success means an evaluator can open the site, scan all weeks at a glance, drill into any week's materials in one click, and immediately find PDFs, assignments, and external resources without friction.

## Brand Personality

Professional, polished, confident. The site should communicate that the training was rigorous and well-structured. The aesthetic is clean and understated — nothing playful or decorative. The content is the signal; the UI frames it without competing.

## Anti-references

- Course platform UIs (Coursera, DataCamp, Udemy) — the site should not look like a generic learning management system with progress bars, badges, or gamification.
- Personal blogs or portfolio sites with whimsical illustrations or casual typography.
- Over-designed agency landing pages with gradient text, glassmorphism, or heavy decorative elements.

## Design Principles

1. **Content first.** The 22 weeks of materials are the reason the site exists. Every layout decision — spacing, hierarchy, motion — serves legibility and scanability. Nothing decorative.
2. **Professional restraint.** Fewer colors, fewer type sizes, fewer moving parts. Polish through precision (alignment, rhythm, spacing) not ornament.
3. **Predictable interactions.** Expanding a card shows its contents. The animation signals what happened without calling attention to itself. The behavior is consistent across all 22 weeks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- All interactive elements must be keyboard accessible.
- Motion must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Color contrast meets WCAG AA (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text).
